FoodieFanatix

Reviews

Steak au Poivre.

Filet mignon, coated with coarsely cracked peppercorns and Dijon mustard. Cooked perfectly. The peppercorns form a crust on the steak and give a balanced contrast to the rich flavour of the steak. With a brandy cream reduction, this was simply delicious.

Beef Cordon Bleu.

This was one item that stood out immediately on the chef’s menu. Envelopes of crumbed beef fillet and rump with rosemary ham, & cheddar cheese filling, on a rich cheese & wine sauce that hit the right spots on every single level.

Line fish.

Golden, caramelised line fish topped with grapefruit and basil was served on a bed of pearl barley with baby marrow and parsley.The dish was bright and fresh thanks to the grapefruit and all the herbs. It’s a light dish which I welcomed with open arms as course.

Chilli Cheese Bomb Burger.

Topped with two slices of cheese, tomato, mayo and jalopeno to give you that kick. The mayo took away some of the heat which was perfect for me.

Dumplings & Ramen.

The ramen was served with soy braised eggs ,spring onion and a delicious hearty miso. You can also choose your own toppings from soy braised pork belly, katsu chicken cheese melt, smoked tofu, black fungus & wakame to pimp your Ramen in the way you like.

Boa.

Absolutely delicious. This little bun is filled with soy braised pork belly along with an amazing Gochujang Mayo that packs a nice tangy punch. Every bite was a delight with spices that delight the senses. Amazing.

Crispy Duck.

The star of the show. Build-Your-Own crispy roasted aromatic duck served with thin pancakes, spring onions, fresh cucumber, and a sweet and sticky side sauce. I always enjoy food being truly satisfying when it’s a combo of textures, temperatures, and flavours (sweet, spicy, salty, acidic, umami) and this dish hit every mark.

Sweet Potato Soup.

Soups are perfect for winter. And I do love a bit of heat. This lightly curried sweet potato soup did the trick for me as a wonderful start to the lunch. Not too hot, but also nothing that would take away from the flavour of the sweet potato, I really enjoyed it. The soup was served with a two slices of fresh coriander toast to add a bit of crunch.

KSH Burger.

You can always taste the difference in quality when you are biting into a grass fed beef burger patty. Juicy and flavourful, a great burger doesn’t need that many toppings. Some fontina cheese doesn’t feel out of place and adds a subtle buttery and nutty flavour while the tomato aioli, pickles, onions and mushroom relish gives the burger a bit more complexity. Simply Delicious. The burger was served with a big portion of thick cut chips on the side.

Classic Cheeseburger.

With a 333g beef patty, cheddar cheese, mayo, lettuce, tomatoes, red onions, and pickles, you’ve got yourself a pure classic burger. What makes this one so special is the blend of the beef patties being used. So flavourful, with the right combination of spices, you don’t need any other fancy toppings to make this an incredibly juicy burger to bite into.
